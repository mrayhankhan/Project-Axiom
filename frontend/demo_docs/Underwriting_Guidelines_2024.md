# Underwriting Guidelines 2024

**Department:** Consumer Lending
**Effective Date:** 2024-01-01
**Version:** 4.0

## 1. Introduction
These guidelines establish the standards for underwriting unsecured personal loans. All credit decisions must adhere to these policies to ensure a balanced risk profile.

## 2. Eligibility Criteria
To be eligible for a loan, an applicant must:
*   Be at least 18 years of age.
*   Be a U.S. citizen or permanent resident.
*   Have a valid Social Security Number.
*   Have a minimum FICO score of 600.

## 3. Income Verification
*   **Salaried Employees:** Two most recent pay stubs.
*   **Self-Employed:** Two years of tax returns (Form 1040).
*   **Retirees:** Award letter or bank statements showing pension/social security deposits.

## 4. Debt-to-Income (DTI) Limits
*   **Maximum DTI:** 43% (including the new loan payment).
*   **Exceptions:** Applicants with a FICO score > 760 may be approved with a DTI up to 50%, subject to manual review by a Senior Underwriter.

## 5. Automatic Declines
Applications meeting any of the following criteria will be automatically declined:
*   Bankruptcy discharged within the last 24 months.
*   Currently past due on any mortgage or rent payment.
*   Utilization rate > 90% on revolving credit lines.

## 6. Override Authority
Exceptions to these guidelines require written approval from a Credit Officer with Level 2 authority or higher. All overrides must be documented in the loan file with a clear justification.
